The provided images illustrate the fractal dimension of Kolkata City across four different years: 1990, 2000, 2010, and 2020. The legend indicates a range of fractal dimensions from 1.00 to 2.00, categorized into five intervals, with red representing the highest fractal dimension (1.57 - 2.00) and dark green representing the lowest (1.00 - 1.40). Over this 30-year period, there is a clear trend of increasing fractal dimension, particularly in the central and northern parts of the city. Areas with high fractal dimensions (red and orange) have expanded significantly from 1990 to 2020, suggesting a consistent increase in the complexity and space-filling nature of urban development. This indicates a growing urban sprawl and a more intricate pattern of land use over time, as the city has become more developed and densely populated.
